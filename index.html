All about me

My name is Susan Badillo! I have a wonderful husband, Andy and 3 wonderful kids Alex, Chelsea, and Nahui! I was raised by my mom Dawn Krause and my dad Gordon Cato. My life has not been a walk in the park but I definitally would not trade it for the world!

I had a rough childhood. I was passed back and forth between my mom and dad. I met the man who became the love of my life at the age 16. We got married and had our first child at 18.

The last couple of years have been the best the best years! As a family we traveled to Mexico twice! The first time we went to Andy's home town Mexico City. The experience our children had was an experience of a lifetime! They were able to meet their cousin for the first time and learn how their daddy grew up! they experienced real street food!

The second time we went to Mexico we went to Isla Mujeres and Playa de Carmen! this was our kids first time going to the ocean! The kids experienced the ocean for the first time and come to find out they are not big fans of salt water!

When there is nothing but good something bad always happens. A year ago my mom was starting to lose he vision. what we had thought to be an effect of her diabetes was actualy a tumor in her brain. 6 months ago she was diagnosed with the tumor putting pressure on her optic nerve causing her vision loss. The good outcome was the tumor was benign. She went in for surgery on March 31st. When they tried to wake her up she was very drowsey and not coming to it mentally. For a week she was in wat we call lala land, saying funny things and sometimes getting aggressive. On April 10th at 12:30 in the morning I recieved a phone call from my sister with the worst news a daughter could get. On the phone, my sister barely able to talk, saying " mom went into cardiac arrest. The doctor was right next to her when it happened they were able to bring her back with 10 minutes of cpr ", but we already knew this was the end, they had to put her life support. A couple hours later, we packed up our car and headed down to Tennessee. On April 11th she was pronounced brain dead.

With everything that has happened in the last few months I have found the way to move on. Yes it still hurts me a lot everyday but I am doing the best I can for my family and my children. My sisters are my world and some days I wish we were closer together and not miles apart. We are continuing to live with her in our minds!

Here's to what the future will bring!
